# Customer Insights Analysis using Excel

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer behavior data from a mall customer dataset to identify meaningful customer segments and generate business insights. Using Microsoft Excel, customer demographics and spending patterns were examined through data segmentation, Pivot Tables, filters, and interactive visualizations.

The objective was to understand how factors such as age, income, and spending behavior influence customer value and to recommend targeted business strategies for improving customer engagement and revenue generation.

---

## Business Problem

Retail businesses often struggle to understand which customers contribute the most value and how different customer groups behave. Without proper customer segmentation, marketing campaigns may be inefficient, resulting in lower customer engagement and reduced return on investment.

The business needed answers to the following questions:

* Which customers represent the highest value to the business?
* Are there customers with high purchasing power who are currently under-engaged?
* How do spending patterns vary across age groups and genders?
* Can customer segments be identified for targeted marketing initiatives?

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains 200 customer records with the following attributes:

* Customer ID
* Gender
* Age
* Annual Income (k$)
* Spending Score (1–100)

The spending score represents customer purchasing behavior and engagement level within the mall.

---

## Analysis Performed

### Data Preparation

* Reviewed dataset for missing values and inconsistencies.
* Validated customer records for data quality.
* Created additional fields for customer segmentation and age grouping.

### Customer Segmentation

Customers were classified into four segments based on Annual Income and Spending Score:

| Segment    | Description                     |
| ---------- | ------------------------------- |
| High Value | High income and high spending   |
| Potential  | High income and lower spending  |
| Impulsive  | Lower income and high spending  |
| Low Value  | Lower income and lower spending |

### Excel Techniques Used

* Pivot Tables
* Pivot Charts
* Slicers and Filters
* IF Functions
* Data Aggregation
* Scatter Plots
* Pie Charts
* Bar and Column Charts
* Dashboard Design

---

## Dashboard Components

The dashboard was designed to provide an interactive view of customer behavior and included:

* Customer Segment Distribution
* Average Spending Score by Gender
* Average Spending Score by Age Group
* Income vs Spending Relationship Analysis
* Average Income by Customer Segment
* Interactive Slicers for Segment, Gender, and Age Group

---

## Key Findings

### 1. High Value Customers Represent the Greatest Revenue Opportunity

Customers with both high income and high spending scores form the most valuable customer segment. These customers are likely to contribute significantly to revenue and should be prioritized for retention initiatives.

### 2. Potential Customers Present Growth Opportunities

A segment of customers demonstrated high income levels but comparatively lower spending behavior. This indicates untapped revenue potential that can be activated through targeted promotions and personalized offers.

### 3. Younger Customers Show Strong Engagement

Analysis revealed that younger age groups generally exhibit higher spending scores compared to older customer groups, suggesting stronger engagement with retail offerings.

### 4. Income Alone Does Not Determine Spending Behavior

The scatter plot analysis showed that several high-income customers spend less than expected, while some lower-income customers exhibit high spending behavior. This highlights the importance of behavioral segmentation over demographic segmentation alone.

### 5. Customer Behavior Differs Across Segments

Distinct spending patterns were observed across customer groups, supporting the need for customized marketing strategies rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

---

## Business Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Develop Loyalty Programs for High Value Customers

Introduce premium membership benefits, exclusive rewards, and personalized experiences to improve retention among high-value customers.

### Recommendation 2: Target Potential Customers with Personalized Promotions

Design campaigns that encourage higher spending among customers with strong purchasing power but lower engagement.

### Recommendation 3: Focus Marketing Efforts on Younger Demographics

Create campaigns aligned with the preferences and purchasing behavior of younger customer groups to maximize engagement.

### Recommendation 4: Implement Segment-Based Marketing

Use customer segmentation to tailor promotions, communication, and offers according to customer behavior patterns.

### Recommendation 5: Monitor Customer Segments Regularly

Establish periodic reviews of customer behavior to identify changing trends and adjust marketing strategies accordingly.
